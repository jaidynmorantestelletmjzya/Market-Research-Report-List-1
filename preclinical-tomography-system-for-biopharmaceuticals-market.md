<p><p>The preclinical tomography system for biopharmaceuticals market involves different types of imaging technologies such as CT (computed tomography), SPECT (single-photon emission computed tomography), and others. CT utilizes X-ray technology to generate cross-sectional images of the body, allowing for detailed visualization of anatomical structures. SPECT, on the other hand, uses radioactive tracers to create three-dimensional images of physiological functions in the body. Lastly, the "others" category encompasses various imaging techniques like PET (positron emission tomography) and MRI (magnetic resonance imaging) that are also used in preclinical tomography systems for biopharmaceutical research.</p></p>
