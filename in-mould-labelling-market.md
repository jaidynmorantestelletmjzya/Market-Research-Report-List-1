<p><p>In Mould Labelling (IML) is a process where pre-printed labels are inserted into the injection moulding machine before the manufacturing process begins. This results in the label becoming an integral part of the final product. IML offers benefits such as high-quality graphics, durability, and resistance to wear and tear. This process is commonly used in packaging, containers, and other plastic products.</p><p>The In Mould Labelling Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.1% during the forecast period. One of the key drivers of this growth is the increasing demand for visually appealing and high-quality packaging in industries such as food and beverage, cosmetics, and automotive. The ability of IML to provide sharp and vibrant graphics attracts consumers and enhances brand visibility. Additionally, the rising focus on sustainability and eco-friendly packaging is also boosting the market for IML, as it allows for the use of recyclable materials and reduced waste.</p><p>The latest trends in the In Mould Labelling Market include advancements in printing technologies, such as digital printing, which offer more flexibility and customization options. Moreover, the integration of smart technologies like QR codes and RFID tags into IML labels is gaining popularity, as it enables brands to interact with consumers and provide valuable information. Overall, the In Mould Labelling Market is set to witness steady growth driven by innovation, consumer preferences, and industry requirements.</p></p>

<p><strong>The In Mould Labelling Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Injection Molding</li><li>Extrusion- Blow Molding</li><li>Thermoforming</li></ul></p>
<p><p>In-Mould Labelling (IML) is a technique used in packaging where the label is placed in the mold before injecting or blowing the plastic material. The Injection Molding market type involves injecting molten plastic into a mold with the label to create a product. Extrusion-Blow Molding market type uses a similar process but with a tube of plastic being extruded and then blown into the mold. Thermoforming market type involves heating a plastic sheet until soft and then shaping it using a mold with the label.</p></p>
