<p><p>Soft tissue core biopsy is a minimally invasive procedure used to collect and analyze small samples of soft tissue in the body. It involves the insertion of a needle or a special device into the soft tissue to obtain a small sample for further analysis. This procedure is commonly used for the diagnosis of cancers, inflammatory diseases, and other conditions affecting the soft tissues.</p><p>The market for soft tissue core biopsy is expected to experience significant growth during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of soft tissue cancers, such as sarcomas, is one of the major factors driving market growth. Additionally, the rising demand for minimally invasive diagnostic procedures and advancements in biopsy techniques are further fueling the market's expansion.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing geriatric population, which is more susceptible to soft tissue diseases, is also contributing to the market's growth. Moreover, favorable reimbursement policies for such diagnostic procedures are providing a boost to market demand.</p><p>In terms of trends, there has been a shift towards the use of image-guided soft tissue core biopsies, such as ultrasound-guided or CT-guided biopsies. These techniques offer better accuracy and precision, reducing the risk of complications and ensuring efficient diagnosis. Another emerging trend is the use of advanced biopsy devices, such as vacuum-assisted biopsy systems, which enable the collection of multiple tissue samples in a single procedure.</p><p>Overall, the soft tissue core biopsy market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years, driven by factors such as increasing prevalence of soft tissue diseases, advancements in biopsy techniques, and favorable reimbursement policies. The market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 8.6% during the forecast period.</p></p>
