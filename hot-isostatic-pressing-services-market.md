<p><p>Hot isostatic pressing (HIP) services encompass various market types, including HIP cladding, HIP brazing, and others. HIP cladding involves the application of a protective layer onto a material's surface through HIP consolidation, enhancing its resistance to corrosion or wear. On the other hand, HIP brazing utilizes HIP technology to join or bond two or more materials, ensuring a strong and durable connection. Lastly, the "Others" category refers to additional applications of HIP, such as densification of materials or sintering, which improve their mechanical properties or remove defects.</p></p>
