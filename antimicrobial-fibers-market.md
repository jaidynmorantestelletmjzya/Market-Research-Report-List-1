<p><p>Antimicrobial fibers can be categorized into two main types: natural antibacterial fibers and chemical antibacterial fibers. Natural antibacterial fibers are materials that possess inherent antimicrobial properties, such as bamboo, wool, and copper-infused textiles. On the other hand, chemical antibacterial fibers are textiles that have been treated with antimicrobial agents during manufacturing, such as polyester or nylon. Both types of antimicrobial fibers are used in various products like clothing, home textiles, and medical supplies to provide protection against harmful bacteria and microbes.</p></p>
