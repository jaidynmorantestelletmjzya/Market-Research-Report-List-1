<p><p>Oncology clinical trials refer to research studies conducted to assess the effectiveness and safety of new cancer treatments, including drugs, therapies, and medical devices. These trials are critical in advancing scientific knowledge of cancer and finding new ways to treat and prevent the disease. They are typically conducted in various phases, ranging from testing the treatment's safety on a small group of individuals to assessing its efficacy in a larger population.</p><p>The oncology clinical trial market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue expanding at a CAGR of 10.4% during the forecast period. With advancements in molecular biology and genomics, researchers are able to identify specific genetic mutations or alterations in tumors, leading to the development of targeted therapies customized to individual patients. This trend is expected to drive the demand for clinical trials focusing on precision medicine.</p><p>Another emerging trend is the adoption of adaptive clinical trial designs. Adaptive trials allow researchers to modify certain aspects of the study while it is ongoing, such as sample size, treatment arms, or patient population.
