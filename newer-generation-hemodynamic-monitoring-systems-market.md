<p><ul><li>Minimally Invasive Hemodynamic Monitoring Systems</li><li>Non-invasive Hemodynamic Monitoring Systems</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Newer-generation hemodynamic monitoring systems can be categorized into two main types: minimally invasive and non-invasive systems. Minimally invasive systems involve inserting a catheter into the patient's blood vessels to directly measure parameters such as blood pressure and cardiac output. On the other hand, non-invasive systems use external sensors to monitor these parameters without the need for invasive procedures. Both types of systems provide crucial real-time data on a patient's cardiovascular function, helping healthcare professionals make informed treatment decisions.</p></p>
