<p><p>Cellular concrete can be classified into four main types based on the materials used in its production: gravel, sand, fly ash, and foaming agents. Gravel-based cellular concrete typically consists of small stones mixed with cement and water. Sand-based cellular concrete utilizes sand as a key ingredient, providing a finer texture. Fly ash-based cellular concrete incorporates fly ash, a byproduct of coal combustion, as a sustainable alternative to cement. Foaming agents are used to create air bubbles within the concrete mixture, resulting in a lightweight and insulating material.</p></p>
