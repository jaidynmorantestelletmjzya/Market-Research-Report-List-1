<p><p>Targeted RNA sequencing (RNA-Seq) is a next-generation sequencing (NGS) technique that selectively sequences a specific subset of RNA molecules in a given sample. It focuses on the analysis of RNA transcripts of interest, providing researchers with valuable insights into gene expression levels, alternative splicing events, and fusion genes. Targeted RNA-Seq offers increased sensitivity, specificity, and cost-effectiveness compared to whole transcriptome RNA-Seq.</p><p>The targeted RNA sequencing market has experienced significant growth over the past few years and is projected to continue this trend in the coming years. Factors contributing to market growth include the rising demand for personalized medicine, advancements in NGS technologies, and the increasing application of targeted RNA-Seq in research and clinical settings.</p><p>Moreover, the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, such as cancer, neurological disorders, and cardiovascular diseases, is also driving the demand for targeted RNA-Seq. This technique enables researchers to identify disease-specific markers, understand disease mechanisms, and develop targeted therapies.</p><p>The market is witnessing several trends that are shaping its growth trajectory. One such trend is the integration of targeted RNA-Seq with other omics technologies, such as DNA sequencing and proteomics, to obtain a comprehensive understanding of biological systems. This integration enables researchers to study complex regulatory networks and identify key biomarkers associated with diseases.</p><p>Another trend is the adoption of cloud-based solutions and bioinformatics tools for data analysis and interpretation. The increasing volume of RNA sequencing data necessitates efficient and scalable methods for data storage, processing, and analysis.</p><p>In conclusion, the targeted RNA sequencing market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.6% during the forecast period. The market growth is driven by factors such as the increasing demand for personalized medicine, advancements in NGS technologies, and the integration of targeted RNA-Seq with other omics technologies. Additionally, the adoption of cloud-based solutions for data analysis and interpretation is a notable trend in the market.</p></p>
