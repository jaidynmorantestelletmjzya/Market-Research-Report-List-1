<p><p>Processed food and beverage preservatives can be categorized into two main types: natural and synthetic. Natural preservatives are derived from plants, animals, or minerals, such as salt, sugar, or vinegar. They help extend shelf life and maintain the freshness of food and beverages. On the other hand, synthetic preservatives are chemicals specifically designed to inhibit the growth of bacteria, yeast, and mold. While both types serve the same purpose of preserving food and beverages, there is a growing consumer preference towards natural preservatives due to health and environmental concerns.</p></p>
