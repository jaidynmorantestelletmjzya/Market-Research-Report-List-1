<p><p>Inert resins are divided into two main types based on their chemical properties and usage in different markets. Alkaline inert resins are used for applications where strong base conditions are required, such as in water treatment, sugar processing, and petrochemical industries. Acidic inert resins, on the other hand, are utilized in acidic environments for processes like ion exchange, metal recovery, and food and beverage production. Both types of inert resins play a vital role in various industries by providing excellent chemical resistance and stability.</p></p>
