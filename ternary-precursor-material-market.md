<p><p>Ternary precursor materials are used in the production of advanced lithium-ion batteries, and there are two main types: NCM (nickel-cobalt-manganese) and NCA (nickel-cobalt-aluminum). NCM type is a popular choice due to its high energy density and good cycling performance, making it suitable for electric vehicles and energy storage systems. On the other hand, NCA type offers even higher energy density but may have slightly lower thermal stability. These materials are crucial in developing more efficient and powerful batteries to meet the growing demand for cleaner energy solutions.</p></p>
