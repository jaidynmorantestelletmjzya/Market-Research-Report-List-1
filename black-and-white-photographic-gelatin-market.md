<p><ul><li>Inert Gelatin</li><li>Semi-inert Gelatin</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Inert gelatin is a type of gelatin used in black and white photography that does not react chemically with light-sensitive silver halide crystals. This makes it suitable for long-term archival purposes as it does not cause any image degradation over time. Semi-inert gelatin, on the other hand, has a slightly higher reactivity with silver halides, allowing for improved image sharpness and tonal range. Both types of gelatin cater to different needs in the black and white photographic market.</p></p>
