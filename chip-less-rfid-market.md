<p><p>Chip-less RFID is a technology used for tracking and identifying items without the need for a traditional integrated circuit (chip). There are two prominent types of chip-less RFID technologies in the market: Surface Acoustic Wave (SAW) and Time Frequency Transient Characteristics (TFTC). </p><p>SAW technology utilizes acoustic waves to encode and decode information, enabling wireless communication between the tag and the reader. TFTC technology, on the other hand, relies on transient characteristics of signals to transmit data. Both these chip-less RFID technologies offer cost-effective and efficient alternatives for item tracking, inventory management, and supply chain optimization.</p></p>
